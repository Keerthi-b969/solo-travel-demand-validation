# Solo Travel Product Prototyping & Demand Validation
## Business Consulting Project
## Project Overview
This project explores how a mid-sized travel company can enter the growing solo travel market through product prototyping and demand validation. It focuses on reducing launch risk through customer research, persona development, prototype package design, and low-cost digital testing methods.
## Problem Statement
The business had established success in group and family travel, but had not yet built clear solo travel offerings despite visible customer interest. The main challenge was the lack of a structured product development process, audience segmentation, and a practical way to test new solo travel ideas before launch.
## Key Findings
- Solo travel is a growing market, especially among professionals aged 35-55.
- Customer research identified key needs including flexibility, safety, connection, wellness, and affordability.
- Customer personas helped align product ideas with user motivations and concerns.
- Prototype travel packages were designed as low-risk concepts for testing demand.
- Digital validation methods such as landing pages, surveys, analytics, and A/B testing were recommended.
## Recommendations
- Launch a small pilot using selected taster packages rather than a full product rollout.
- Use targeted email campaigns and persona-based messaging to test interest more effectively.
- Prioritise packages with stronger engagement signals such as clicks, sign-ups, and lower bounce rates.
- Position solo travel as an empowering and flexible experience while addressing concerns around safety and connection.
- Build an ongoing feedback and testing process for future product development.
